LIBRARY DIRECTIVE

FIRST PART
Purpose, Scope and Definitions

Aim
ARTICLE 1-

  • This directive regulates the duties, authorities and responsibilities of Istanbul Aydin University Libraries, their working principles, the conditions under which users benefit from the libraries, their responsibilities and their coordination with other educational units.

Scopre
ARTICLE 2-

  • This directive covers the IAU Central Library and Bahçelievler Library.

Definitions
ARTICLE 3- Abbreviations used in this directive:

  1. University: Istanbul Aydin University,
  2. Library” means the library of Istanbul Aydin University,
  3. Rector: The Rector of Istanbul Aydin University,
  4. Rectorate: Istanbul Aydin University Administration,
  5. System: Library automation system,
  6. Member: Academic and administrative staff and students working at IAU,
  7. User: Persons who benefit from Library services and information resources,regardless of whether they are members or not,
  8. Banking Unit: The place where long-term lending transactions are carried out,
  9. Exchange student: Students who come to the University for a certain period of time through international exchange programs (such as Socrates, Erasmus),
  10. ILL: (Inter Library Loan) Interlibrary loan transactions,
  11. Information resources: Printed (books, magazines, newspapers, reports, etc.) and electronic (online databases, CDs, DVDs, tapes, etc.) resources in the library collection,
  12. Transaction cost: The cost calculated for the transactions made in the process of providing information resources,
Senate Date of Acceptance and Decision Number: 14.09.2022 Date and 2022-12 / 7

Organization and Management
ARTICLE 4 - The coordination, management and services of the library are carried out by the following bodies:

  1. The Coordination Board consists of one faculty member or staff member elected by the units affiliated to IAU for two years. The Head of Department is a natural member of the coordination board. When necessary, the board meets under the chairmanship of the Rector or a faculty member representing the Rector.
  2. Department Head Office

Duties and powers of library bodies
ARTICLE 5-

  1. The Coordination Board makes recommendations to the Department Head regarding the determination of the Library's goals and the coordination between the units and the library.
  2. The Department Head Office determines the principles of the services to be provided by the library and ensures their operation in line with the recommendations of the Coordination Board. It submits the decisions taken in this regard to the approval of the Rectorate. It ensures the execution of all library services. It ensures and supervises the coordination between the branch libraries affiliated to the central library.

Library services
ARTICLE 6-

  1. Collection development: Providing printed and electronic publications through purchase, subscription, donation and exchange in order to support the education and training provided at the university, to contribute to research and to support the social and cultural needs of users. Academic staff and academicians are asked for publications they would like to receive in the library at six-month intervals. They are evaluated by the Collection Development Department. The following principles are taken into consideration when evaluating requests:
  2. The subject of the requested publication (compliance with IAU's education and training policy)
  3. Interdisciplinary use
  4. Up-to-dateness
  5. The nature of the publishing house
  6. The proportion of the subject of the publication within the collection.
  7. Price
  8. Language of Publication
  9. Physical properties (printed, electronic, microform, etc.)
  10. Whether the exact copy of the requested publication is available in IAU Libraries,
  11. If the lost publications cannot be found within a certain period of time, they are purchased again according to the usage rate and demand.
  12. Publications that are physically unavailable should be re-purchased based on usage rate and demand.
  13. In electronic resource subscriptions, the scientific nature of the database, its subject, license conditions, ease of use and usage statistics are taken into account.

As a result of the evaluation, the provision of the resources decided to be purchased is carried out by the Purchasing Unit. Purchased resources are added to the library system and receive an asset number. Resources to be used as course materials are loaned to academic staff for a semester period.

  1. Technical services: All the work done until all publications provided by the collection development department are made available to the user. Cataloging and classification; all materials provided to libraries are organized according to contemporary cataloging and classification systems and are offered to the service of the users.
  2. User Services: These are services provided for the benefit of users from information resources and library services. User services are carried out by expert librarians with sufficient numbers and qualifications to ensure that these services are carried out efficiently.
  1. Consulting services are the entirety of the work carried out to introduce library resources and services to library users, provide training for the efficient use of library resources, provide literature search services, announce new publications, and effectively carry out information and document services.
  2. Lending services; to ensure and supervise the circulation of all publications in the library within and outside the library within the framework of the principles set out in this directive, in order to meet the educational, research, scientific activity and cultural needs of IAU members.
  3. Interlibrary loan service for publications/documents; is the provision of information resources that IAU members need but are not available in IAU Libraries, by borrowing or photocopying from domestic and foreign libraries. The rules regarding this service are determined separately by the Department Head and come into force with the approval of the Rector.
  4. Training and orientation services; To organize application and internship programs for students studying in information and document management departments of universities, in-service training programs for library personnel, and user training programs for students and faculty members to ensure efficient use of library resources and services.

SECOND PART
Library Terms of Use

Members
ARTICLE 4-

  1. IAU academic, administrative staff, undergraduate, associate degree and graduate students are the permanent members of the Library.
  2. Members are responsible for notifying the library in a timely manner of any changes in the information provided in the registration form.

External Membership Conditions

Every user who becomes a member is deemed to have committed to complying with the rules of the IAU Library. The membership card must be shown at every entrance to the library. All memberships are valid for 1 year. If a member is requested again, the membership procedures are renewed.

    Daily External user:
    Can visit the library daily. Can use the library collection in the library (photocopy etc.) Cannot borrow materials. Library usage period is 08.00-17.00 for 7 days. Must show ID card at the entrance and get permission from the officer. Fills out the information form prepared for external visitors (institution they come from, reason for visit etc.).

    ARTICLE 6-

    • All users, regardless of whether they are members of the library or not, agree to comply with the Library Directive.

    ARTICLE 7-

    • The user is obliged to comply with the 'Law on Intellectual and Artistic Works' when using information resources. The user cannot impose any legal liability on the University due to his/her opposition to this article.

    THIRD PART
    Lending Rules

    ARTICLE 8-

    1. Members must show their university or institution ID cards when borrowing information resources. Staff who lose their ID cards must bring a document from the Personnel Department; students must bring a document from the Student Affairs Department.
    2. Information resources cannot be borrowed and extensions cannot be made with another member's ID card.
    3. A user who has an overdue information source cannot borrow a new information source or extend the period without returning the information source and paying the overdue penalty.
    4. The library reserves the right to request the return of borrowed information sources without waiting for the return date when deemed necessary. After the user is notified, the information source must be returned within 3 (three) days at the latest.
    5. Periodicals, reference books (encyclopedia, dictionary, almanac, etc.), master's and doctoral theses and rare works cannot be borrowed
    6. Before the return date of borrowed information sources, a warning note is sent to the member's e-mail address by the system. However, the user is responsible for following the return dates of information sources borrowed from the library. Failure to receive the warning note sent by the system for any reason is not considered a valid reason for not applying the sanctions resulting from the delay.
    7. A reminder letter will be sent 3 (three) times for information sources that are borrowed and not returned on time.
    8. Information about members is not given to other users.

    CHAPTER FOUR
    Principles Regarding the Rules

    ARTICLE 9-

    • The Library and Documentation Department notifies the Rectorate of any proposed changes to the rules at the beginning of each academic year. Changes are made by decision of the University Senate and come into force as of the beginning of that academic year.

    LENDING RULES:
    Academic and Administrative Staff

    1. Academic staff can borrow a total of 5 books for 20 days and any resources they want for a semester if they are intended for use as course materials. If the information source has not been reserved by another user; they can extend the loan 3 times for the books they borrow for 20 days. They cannot extend the term for the textbooks they borrow for a semester. If they do not borrow the information sources they have reserved within 3 days; the reservation is canceled by the system.
    2. Administrative staff can borrow 5 books for 20 days. If they have not been reserved by another user; they can extend the period for the books 3 times. They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.

    Student

    1. A graduate student can borrow 5 books for 15 days. He/she can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If he/she does not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.
    2. Undergraduate students can borrow 3 books for 15 days. If it has not been reserved by another user; they can extend the period 3 times. They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.
    3. Associate degree students can borrow 3 books for 15 days. If it has not been reserved by another user; they can extend the period 3 times. They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.

    Other Users

    1. Exchange students can use the library within the scope of the conditions of use of IAU students.
    2. Academic and administrative staff using the Library through the Interlibrary Loan (ILL) system can borrow 3 books within the period in accordance with the lending rules of the other institution. They cannot extend the period. They cannot reserve information resources on other users. They cannot borrow journals and books in the Reserve Collection. IAU academic and administrative staff who want to borrow books from other libraries are obliged to comply with the lending rules of the relevant library.

    Rules to be applied in case of Delay, Loss and Damage:

    1. In case of delay in returning the information resources borrowed from the Library's Reception Unit, a fine of 1 TL per day per information resource will be charged, between 1 and 40 days.
    2. Users who damage information sources for any reason inside or outside the library are asked to provide a new copy of the information source. If the user cannot provide the information source; if it is out of print, item c applies, if it is still in print, a 10 TL transaction fee is added to the current price of the information source and the fee is collected
    3. In case of loss or damage of out-of-print books, if the market value of the information source can be determined; the TL equivalent of the currency in which it will be procured is calculated based on the Central Bank foreign exchange sales rate on the day the Library is operating.
    4. In case of loss of CD-ROMs with book supplements, the user will be charged a fine of 10 TL, and in case of loss of DVD-ROMs, a fine of 30 TL.

    CHAPTER FIVE
    Use of Electronic Resources

    ARTICLE 10-

    1. Only IAU faculty members and students can access electronic resources from inside and outside the campus.
    2. The publication cannot be copied from databases, by using robots or similar software, by downloading, or systematically transferred to personal computers or external memories.
    3. Documents such as articles, books, theses, etc. copied from databases, in whole or in part, cannot be distributed via data storage tools or e-mail lists and cannot be sent to persons or institutions outside the University.
    4. Users who do not comply with the above rules will be reported to the Rectorate for necessary legal action to be taken.

    CHAPTER SIX
    Rules to be followed in the library

    ARTICLE 11-

    1. Food and drink are not allowed in the library building,
    2. Mobile phones are kept off inside the library building.
    3. No unauthorized recordings can be made with cameras, photo machines, mobile phones, etc. in the library
    4. Users are responsible for their own belongings. The Library and Documentation Department is not responsible for lost or stolen items.
    5. No loud talking or group work is allowed in the reader's rooms and corridors.
    6. Borrowing and returning transactions are completed 15 minutes before the library closes.
    7. Computers in the library are used only for educational and research purposes.
    8. In case of non-compliance with library rules, the user is obliged to take into account the warnings of the library staff.
    9. Any user who damages the library information resources and belongings (computer, table, chair, etc.) in any way will be reported to the Rector's Office for the necessary legal action to be taken.
    10. In case of unauthorized removal or attempt to remove information resources from the Library, the user who engages in this action will be reported to the Rector's Office for the necessary legal action to be taken.

    CHAPTER SEVEN
    Rules to be Applied in Case of Members Leaving the University

    ARTICLE 12-

    1. The severance process of members who leave the University for retirement, resignation or other reasons is not completed until they return the information sources they borrowed and pay any fines, if any.
    2. Students' registration renewal, graduation and termination procedures cannot be carried out without returning the information sources and paying the penalty fee, if any.
    3. Any member who leaves the University for any reason without returning the borrowed information source will be reported to the Rector's Office for legal action to be taken.

    CHAPTER EIGHT
    Donation Policy

    Senate Date of Acceptance and Decision Number: 14.09.2022 Date and 2022-12 / 7

    ARTICLE 13-

    • Our library uses the "Donation" channel in addition to "Purchasing" among the collection development methods. Materials donated to our library are evaluated and accepted within the scope of the collection development policy.
    • IAU Information Center accepts library materials (books, periodicals, audio-visual materials, etc.) from individuals and legal entities that comply with the purpose and principles of the university library in order to support education and training at the university, contribute to research, and develop the library collection in line with the cultural needs of the users.

    The IAU Library takes the following into consideration when accepting any printed or electronic material as a donation.

    1. Upon the application made to the Information Center by those wishing to make a donation, a committee consisting of two or three librarians and structured under the Acquisitions Department is assigned by the Department to examine and examine the donation in order to examine the collection to be donated and to carry out the necessary technical procedures.
    2. Information resources to be donated are delivered to the Library with a “Donation Delivery Report”. The Department Head has the authority to decide whether donated information resources will be added to the library collection, or whether those not added will be donated to other institutions or sent for recycling. Materials such as books that are not accepted because they are not up-to-date in terms of content, do not have historical value or are not suitable for the purpose of the university library can be evaluated in another type of library. Donors may be guided by the committee on this matter.
    3. Donations are accepted to the Library only after the committee examines the collection and finds it appropriate. If the resources are suitable for the library collection, technical procedures are carried out and accession numbers are given, and they are added to the collection like other materials and made available for the use of users. The priority for the necessary procedures to make donated books etc. ready for service is in line with the library's supply policy. In accordance with the supply policy, the priority is given to books etc. purchased with the requests of users such as teaching staff, students etc. in the procedures.
    4. The library may also apply to private or legal entities for collection donations.
    5. The materials to be accepted as donations must be up-to-date and scientifically sound, suitable for the education provided at the university, or supportive of researchers in social and cultural terms.
    6. Materials that are for ideological and religious propaganda purposes, that are incompatible with moral rules, that contain propaganda for terrorist organisations, or that are found to be insulting to national and religious feelings will not be accepted.
    7. Multi-volume works such as encyclopedias must be integrated. Materials that do not have an integrated volume (books, periodicals, encyclopedias) are not included in the collection. In order for periodicals to be accepted as donations, the beginning or continuation of the collection must be in the library.
    8. Materials such as books, periodicals, etc. that are worn out to the point of being unusable, have torn bindings, torn pages, or missing pages will not be accepted as donations.
    9. Manuscripts and rare works accepted as donations are kept in their own section.
    10. Donors of between 1500-3000 books are registered as silver donors, donors of between 3000-5000 books are registered as gold donors, and donors of 5000 and above books are registered as platinum donors on the Donation Board. A separate section is created for donors of 7,500 books and above.
    11. If the donated publication is available in the library collection (except for reserve publications), it will not be accepted.
    12. Special space requests from donors or institutions will not be accepted in order not to disrupt the shelf integrity of the collection. Donated publications cannot be requested to be kept in a separate section from the collection or together on the same shelf.
    13. The Department Head has the right to return the materials it accepts as donations or to donate them to another library if it deems appropriate
    14. Donors cannot later claim any rights or requests regarding their donations.
    15. Resources such as books, magazines, etc. that belong to or are published by the institution are not accepted as donations.
    16. The donor may request a list of the donated materials after the technical processes of the books are completed.
    17. If donors wish, they can have a seal made with their name and surname indicating that they have made a donation to the University Library and submit it to the library. This seal is printed on the inside cover of the works donated by the library. The name of the donor and the date of donation are recorded in the automation program used.

    Materials that will not be included in the collection as a donation :

    1. Primary and secondary education textbooks (excluding current Ministry of National Education textbooks)
    2. Course notes (including current and past)
    3. Information sources reproduced by pirated or photocopied means etc.
    4. Duplicate ; materials for which no second or more copies are required
    5. Materials such as activity reports, statistics, etc. issued by universities (excluding IAU Publications )

    THIRD PART
    Lending Rules

    ARTICLE 8-

    1. Members must show their university or institution ID cards when borrowing information resources. Staff who lose their ID cards must bring a document from the Personnel Department; students must bring a document from the Student Affairs Department.
    2. Information resources cannot be borrowed and extensions cannot be made with another member's ID card.
    3. A user who has an overdue information source cannot borrow a new information source or extend the period without returning the information source and paying the overdue penalty.
    4. The library reserves the right to request the return of borrowed information sources without waiting for the return date when deemed necessary. After the user is notified, the information source must be returned within 3 (three) days at the latest.
    5. Periodicals, reference books (encyclopedia, dictionary, almanac, etc.), master's and doctoral theses and rare works cannot be borrowed
    6. Before the return date of borrowed information sources, a warning note is sent to the member's e-mail address by the system. However, the user is responsible for following the return dates of information sources borrowed from the library. Failure to receive the warning note sent by the system for any reason is not considered a valid reason for not applying the sanctions resulting from the delay.
    7. A reminder letter will be sent 3 (three) times for information sources that are borrowed and not returned on time.
    8. Information about members is not given to other users.

    Principles Regarding the Rules

    1. Academic staff can borrow a total of 5 books for 20 days and any resources they want for a semester if they are intended for use as course materials. They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.

They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.
    3. Associate degree students can borrow 3 books for 15 days. If it has not been reserved by another user; they can extend the period 3 times. They can reserve 3 information sources through the system. If they do not borrow the reserved information sources within 3 days; the reservation process is canceled by the system.

    Donation Policy

    Senate Date of Acceptance and Decision Number: 14.09.2022 Date and 2022-12 / 7

    ARTICLE 13-

    • Our library uses the "Donation" channel in addition to "Purchasing" among the collection development methods. Materials donated to our library are evaluated and accepted within the scope of the collection development policy.
    • IAU Information Center accepts library materials (books, periodicals, audio-visual materials, etc.) from individuals and legal entities that comply with the purpose and principles of the university library in order to support education and training at the university, contribute to research, and develop the library collection in line with the cultural needs of the users.

    The IAU Library takes the following into consideration when accepting any printed or electronic material as a donation.

    1. Upon the application made to the Information Center by those wishing to make a donation, a committee consisting of two or three librarians and structured under the Acquisitions Department is assigned by the Department to examine and examine the donation in order to examine the collection to be donated and to carry out the necessary technical procedures.
    2. Information resources to be donated are delivered to the Library with a “Donation Delivery Report”. The Department Head has the authority to decide whether donated information resources will be added to the library collection, or whether those not added will be donated to other institutions or sent for recycling. Materials such as books that are not accepted because they are not up-to-date in terms of content, do not have historical value or are not suitable for the purpose of the university library can be evaluated in another type of library. Donors may be guided by the committee on this matter.
    3. Donations are accepted to the Library only after the committee examines the collection and finds it appropriate. If the resources are suitable for the library collection, technical procedures are carried out and accession numbers are given, and they are added to the collection like other materials and made available for the use of users. The priority for the necessary procedures to make donated books etc. ready for service is in line with the library's supply policy. In accordance with the supply policy, the priority is given to books etc. purchased with the requests of users such as teaching staff, students etc. in the procedures.
    4. The library may also apply to private or legal entities for collection donations.
    5. The materials to be accepted as donations must be up-to-date and scientifically sound, suitable for the education provided at the university, or supportive of researchers in social and cultural terms.
    6. Materials that are for ideological and religious propaganda purposes, that are incompatible with moral rules, that contain propaganda for terrorist organisations, or that are found to be insulting to national and religious feelings will not be accepted.
    7. Multi-volume works such as encyclopedias must be integrated. Materials that do not have an integrated volume (books, periodicals, encyclopedias) are not included in the collection. In order for periodicals to be accepted as donations, the beginning or continuation of the collection must be in the library.
    8. Materials such as books, periodicals, etc. that are worn out to the point of being unusable, have torn bindings, torn pages, or missing pages will not be accepted as donations.
    9. Manuscripts and rare works accepted as donations are kept in their own section.
    10. Donors of between 1500-3000 books are registered as silver donors, donors of between 3000-5000 books are registered as gold donors, and donors of 5000 and above books are registered as platinum donors on the Donation Board. A separate section is created for donors of 7,500 books and above.
    11. If the donated publication is available in the library collection (except for reserve publications), it will not be accepted.
    12. Special space requests from donors or institutions will not be accepted in order not to disrupt the shelf integrity of the collection. Donated publications cannot be requested to be kept in a separate section from the collection or together on the same shelf.
    13. The Department Head has the right to return the materials it accepts as donations or to donate them to another library if it deems appropriate
    14. Donors cannot later claim any rights or requests regarding their donations.
    15. Resources such as books, magazines, etc. that belong to or are published by the institution are not accepted as donations.
    16. The donor may request a list of the donated materials after the technical processes of the books are completed.
    17. If donors wish, they can have a seal made with their name and surname indicating that they have made a donation to the University Library and submit it to the library. This seal is printed on the inside cover of the works donated by the library. The name of the donor and the date of donation are recorded in the automation program used.

    Materials that will not be included in the collection as a donation :

    1. Primary and secondary education textbooks (excluding current Ministry of National Education textbooks)
    2. Course notes (including current and past)
    3. Information sources reproduced by pirated or photocopied means etc.
    4. Duplicate ; materials for which no second or more copies are required
    5. Materials such as activity reports, statistics, etc. issued by universities (excluding IAU Publications )
