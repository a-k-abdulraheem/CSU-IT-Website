Borrowing Conditions

Associate and Undergraduate Degrees

Degree

Academic and Administrative

  • In case of loss of borrowed material, the user is obliged to bring an exact copy of the material. If an exact copy cannot be found, the cost of the material and the processing fee will be collected from the user.
  • A user who wants to borrow a material borrowed by someone else can do so by accessing the library web page over the internet or from the web. However, if the user does not return the reserved material for some reason, the library is not responsible. Reserved material returned to the library must be collected from the lending desk within 7 days. Materials not collected within this period are placed back on the shelves.
  • It is mandatory to borrow each item that will be taken out of the library.
  • If the borrowed books are lost, have missing pages or are damaged, the user is responsible for this situation. The user is responsible for getting a new book that they have lost or damaged as soon as possible. If it is not possible to obtain it, they must pay the current value of the book. The personnel on duty at the lending desk do not have any authority to cancel or postpone late payment fines.
  • If the item is not on the shelf and has not been borrowed by another user, assistance is requested from the circulation desk. When the item is found, the reader is notified and it is kept in the circulation section for 7 days.
  • The Library reserves the right to recall a publication if deemed necessary.
  • Reference sources (encyclopedia, dictionary, etc.), periodicals, and non-printed theses cannot be borrowed.
  • Users must have a valid e-mail address, as the library sends informational notes to users via e-mail, such as due dates, overdue items, and the receipt of reserved items.
  • Upon graduation or in the event that their relationship with the school is terminated for any reason, students must return the borrowed publications to the library and obtain a "no relationship" document from the library.
